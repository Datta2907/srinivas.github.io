Lost & Found App

A Human-Centered Design Project

Description & Motivation

This project aims to solve the recurring problem of misplaced items across campus. The motivation comes from understanding student frustration with the existing lost-item workflow. Our goal was to create a seamless experience that reduces effort, anxiety, and uncertainty.

Online Study

We did on online study on the existing systems and how are they working and helping students. there are some apps which were specific to universities. we gathered those app details from playstore and appstore, performed review scrapping and found out what people think of those apps.

Prototyping Phase

From the review scrapping we found out that there 2 types of apps: one is dependent on AI and other is pureply human. so 2 Low‚Äëfidelity prototypes were created to visualize the workflow and gather initial feedback.

User Study 1 Results

In the first user study, participants evaluated the initial prototypes. Key findings:

  • Prior Experience Shapes Motivation
  • Task 2 (Report Found Item) is Critical
  • Privacy is Important but Not Primary
  • Color Scheme and Branding Matter
  • Hybrid Approach Suggested

User Study 2 Results

The second study tested the refined prototype. Improvements validated:

  • Return Method Flexibility is Highly Valued
  • Privacy Controls Provide Confidence
  • Building Location Guidance is Critical
  • Visual Design Supports NMSU Identity
  • Search and Filter Features Are Essential
  • Task Completion Was Intuitive
